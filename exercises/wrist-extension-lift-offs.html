Kneeling Wrist Extensions

Wrist mobility, forearm strength, hand health

Minimum Effective Dose
1 min
The shortest time that still creates real, measurable change. Hit the dose and the benefit starts. Everything after is bonus.

Why it works

Modern life wrecks wrists through typing, phones, and driving. Kneeling wrist extensions build extension range and forearm strength simultaneously, protecting against repetitive strain injuries and carpal tunnel symptoms.

How to do it

Lift clean, don't claw.
Tension through the top of the forearm. Fingers stay on the floor. Lift from the wrist only, not a finger curl.
  • Kneel with palms flat on the floor, fingers pointing toward you
  • Lift your palms off the floor while keeping fingers down. Tension through the forearm
  • Lower with control, lifting from the wrist rather than curling the fingers
  • The tension should be through the forearm, not the finger joints

Variants

Easier
Less weight forward.
Stay back on the knees. Gentle load on wrists.
Harder
More weight forward. Pause at top.
Lean into it. Hold the lift for 2 seconds.
