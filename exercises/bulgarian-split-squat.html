Bulgarian Split Squat

Quads, glutes, single-leg strength, balance

Minimum Effective Dose
40s
The shortest time that still creates real, measurable change. Hit the dose and the benefit starts. Everything after is bonus.

Why it works

The Bulgarian split squat is the most effective single-leg strength exercise. Elevating the rear foot shifts nearly all the load onto the front leg, building unilateral strength that transfers directly to running, climbing, and injury prevention.

How to do it

Rear foot elevated. Front knee tracks.
Use a chair or step. Lower until back knee nears floor.
  • Place your rear foot on a chair or step behind you
  • Lower your back knee toward the floor
  • Front knee tracks over your toes
  • Drive through the front heel to stand

Variants

Easier
Lower rear foot. Hand on wall.
Use a low step. Support for balance.
Harder
Pause at the bottom. 3 seconds.
No bounce out of the hole. Hold and drive.
