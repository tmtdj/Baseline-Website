The pattern

Streaks aren't motivation.
They're a debt collector.

A streak works for the first two weeks. You feel proud. You log in. The number ticks up. Then life happens. You miss a day. The number resets to zero. And quietly, in the back of your mind, you decide the whole thing was a waste.

That isn't motivation. That's a behavioural trap built by growth teams to inflate retention metrics. It works on paper. It does not work for you.

A different design

A clean slate every Monday.
No score to protect.

Baseline shows you a simple grid for the week. You either filled today or you didn't. There's no number that grows or resets. Nothing to lose. Miss Tuesday and Wednesday is exactly the same as it would have been. Miss the whole week and Monday comes anyway.

It turns out that when you remove the punishment, people show up more often, not less. Because the cost of returning is zero.

What's removed

Things Baseline does not have.

No streak counter
There is no number tracking how many days in a row you have shown up. There is nothing to lose by missing a day.
No leaderboard
No comparison to other users. No public profile. No friends list. Your fitness is not a competition.
No guilt notifications
We don't ping you to say you've fallen behind. The only notification is an optional reminder if you ask for one.
No challenges
No 28-day reset. No 75 hard. No December push. Movement is for life, not for a calendar window.
No score
No readiness score. No recovery score. No fitness age. Nothing to optimise. You either moved today or you didn't.
No social feed
No likes. No followers. No kudos. No public workout history.
