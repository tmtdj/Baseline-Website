The pattern

The app is the hook.
The coach is the product.

MovesMethod's business model is the funnel. You download the app, do a few sessions, and the nudges toward a paid 1-on-1 coach start. That's not a side feature, it's how the company makes money. If you want that coaching relationship, it's a legitimate offer.

But most people looking for a mobility app don't want a coach. They want to move their body for 15 minutes a day without being sold anything. That's not what MovesMethod is built for.

FAQ

MovesMethod vs BaselineBody.

Is BaselineBody a good MovesMethod alternative?

If you want daily mobility without a course to complete, a coach to follow, or a tier to upgrade to, BaselineBody is a simpler MovesMethod alternative. One session a day, under 20 minutes, one flat price.

Does BaselineBody include 1-on-1 coaching like MovesMethod?

No. BaselineBody has no coaching tier, no upsell, and no human trainer. It is a self-guided app with one price: $9.99/yr. MovesMethod offers application-only 1-on-1 coaching as a paid upgrade.

How much does BaselineBody cost compared to MovesMethod?

BaselineBody is $9.99/yr, flat. MovesMethod has tiered pricing: a $27 one-time Toolkit, a Club subscription at $324 per year ($97 intro), plus 1-on-1 coaching priced by application.
