Honest take

When Strong is the better choice.

If you train at a gym three or more times a week and you want a clean, fast, well designed logger to track progressive overload, Strong is excellent. There is nothing better at what it does.

Baseline is for the other person. The one who downloaded Strong, logged six sessions, then opened it once a month. If logging is a chore, the answer isn't a better logger. It's an app that doesn't need one.

FAQ

Strong vs Baseline.

Is Baseline a good Strong app alternative?

If you want a daily workout without logging every set, Baseline is a Strong app alternative designed around that. The session is built for you, there is nothing to log, and it includes mobility and breathwork that Strong doesn't cover.

Do I need to bring my own program to Baseline?

No. Strong requires you to bring your own program and log every set. Baseline builds today's session for you. You press start and follow the guide.

Does Baseline include mobility and breathwork?

Yes. Every Baseline session blends mobility, bodyweight strength, and breathwork in under 20 minutes. Strong is a workout logger with no mobility or breathwork content.
