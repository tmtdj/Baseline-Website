Health Disclaimer

Last updated: February 2026

Not medical advice

Baseline does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. The exercises, breathing techniques, and movement sessions offered through the app are for general wellness purposes only and are not a substitute for professional medical guidance.

You should consult your physician or other qualified healthcare provider before beginning any exercise programme, particularly if you have a history of heart disease, high blood pressure, respiratory conditions, joint problems, or any other medical condition that could be affected by physical activity.

Exercise at your own risk

Physical activity carries inherent risks, including but not limited to musculoskeletal injury, cardiovascular events, and overexertion. By using Baseline, you acknowledge that you participate in all physical activities entirely at your own risk.

If you experience faintness, dizziness, pain, shortness of breath, or any other concerning symptoms during a session, stop immediately and seek medical attention.

Baseline is not a personal trainer, physiotherapist, or medical professional. The app generates movement sessions algorithmically based on general exercise principles. It cannot assess your individual physical condition, limitations, or readiness for exercise.

Breathing exercises: important safety information

Baseline includes breathing techniques that intentionally alter your respiratory pattern. Some modes involve extended breath holds, controlled hyperventilation, and autonomic nervous system activation. These techniques can cause lightheadedness, tingling, visual changes, and in rare cases, loss of consciousness.

Breathing: general precautions

Always practise breathing exercises while seated or lying down in a safe environment. Never practise breathing techniques while driving, operating machinery, in or near water, at heights, or in any situation where loss of consciousness could result in injury.

Breathing exercises that involve breath retention or rapid breathing are not recommended if you have a history of:

Cardiovascular disease or abnormal blood pressure · Epilepsy or seizure disorders · Respiratory conditions including asthma or COPD · Stroke or transient ischaemic attacks · Pregnancy · Panic disorder or severe anxiety · Retinal detachment or glaucoma · Recent surgery · Any condition where changes in blood pressure, blood oxygen, or carbon dioxide levels could pose a risk.

If you are taking any medication, particularly for cardiovascular, respiratory, or neurological conditions, consult your doctor before using the breathing features of this app.

Override mode: elevated risk

Baseline's Override mode is an advanced breathing protocol that involves a sustained cycle of deep breathing followed by an extended breath retention of up to 90 seconds and a recovery hold. This technique produces significant physiological changes including temporary reductions in blood oxygen, shifts in blood pH, and activation of the sympathetic nervous system.

Override is not suitable for everyone. The risks described above are amplified in this mode. Fainting is a known possibility during or immediately after the breath retention phase. This is why the app requires you to acknowledge a safety disclaimer before your first use of Override, and why you should always practise it while lying down in a safe, clear space.

Do not attempt Override in water, while standing, while driving, or in any unsupervised environment where loss of consciousness could lead to injury or drowning.

Children and minors

Baseline is designed for adults aged 18 and over. The app is not intended for use by children or minors. The breathing exercises, particularly Override mode, involve physiological techniques that are not suitable for anyone under 18.
